Elements of supergeometry#

Definition: A supermanifold $\mathcal{M}$ is a locally ringed space $(M,\mathcal{O}_{M})$ which locally looks like

$$ \left( \mathcal{U},C^\infty(\mathcal{U}) \otimes \bigwedge V^{*} \right) $$

where $\mathcal{U} \subset \mathbb{R}^{n}$ is open and $V$ is a finite dimensional real vector space. $M$ is called the body of $\mathcal{M}$ and $\mathcal{O}_{M}$ the structure sheaf.

A supervector field is a vector field $X$ on a supermanfold $\mathcal{M}$, with local coordinates $(x^{i},\theta^{\mu})$, which is given by

$$ X = \sum_{i,\mu} X^{i}\partial_{x^{i}} + Y^{\mu}\partial_{\theta^{\mu}} $$

Example: Let $\mathcal{N}$ be a $(k|n-k)$-supermanifold and let $\Psi \in C^\infty(\mathcal{N})_{\mathrm{odd}}$ an odd function. One has a Lagrangian

$$ \begin{align} \mathcal{N}_{\Psi} &:= \mathrm{graph}(d\Psi) \subset \Pi T^{*}\mathcal{N} \\ \mathcal{F}^{\partial}_{\partial N} &= \underbrace{\mathfrak{g}\otimes \Omega^{1}(\partial N)[1]}_{\ni A} \oplus \underbrace{\mathfrak{g} \otimes \Omega^{n-2}(\partial N) [n-2]}_{\ni B} \oplus \underbrace{\mathfrak{g} \otimes \Omega^{0} (\partial N) [1]}_{\ni c} \oplus \underbrace{\mathfrak{g}\otimes \Omega^{n-2}(N)[n-2]}_{\ni A^{+}} \end{align} $$

The boundary action and its corresponding Hamiltonian vector field with respect to $\omega^{\partial}_{\partial N}$ are given by

$$ \begin{aligned} \mathcal{S}^{\partial}_{\partial N} &= \int_{\partial N} \mathrm{tr} \left( B \mathrm{d}_{A}c + \frac{1}{2}A^{+}[c,c] \right) \\ Q^{\partial}_{\partial N} &= \int_{\partial N} \mathrm{tr} \left( \mathrm{d}_{A}c \frac{\delta}{\delta A} + [B,c] \frac{\delta}{\delta B} + (\mathrm{d}_{A}B + [A^{+},c])\frac{\delta}{\delta A^{+}} + \frac{1}{2}[c,c]\frac{\delta}{\delta c} \right) \end{aligned} $$
